Hiragana & Katakana In One Day

  2017-11-05  -    Voylin's Life, Learn Japanese

Hiragana and Katakana in a single day!

Let’s learn Hiragana and Katakana today!!! This is the first big step you should take when starting with learning Japanese and once you know them, you don’t have to review often because they are used in all sorts of texts, books and apps for learning Japanese!

Site: Tanoshii Japanese

Hiragana? Katakana?

Hiragana and Katakana are two of the three writing systems used in the Japanese language, the last one being Kanji. While learning to read and write Japanese can look quite impossible, it is actually possible to learn Hiragana and Katakana in one day with the right approach and mindset. This will allow you to read certain texts which don’t include too much kanji. Let’s explore the benefits of learning Hiragana and Katakana and discuss some tips and tricks to help you master these scripts in just one day.

Learning Hiragana and Katakana is a key step in mastering the Japanese language. All Japanese writing systems are used together. To know when to use what writing system, Katakana is for the foreign words used in Japanese, Hiragana and Kanji is used for the rest. Kanji is used to group certain Hiragana sounds together to make a text easier to read as kanji gives a better image of the meaning of each word.

Some tips!

  1. Start with the basics: Before diving into learning Hiragana and Katakana, it’s important to familiarize yourself with the basic shapes and sounds of the characters. Katakana is sharp and Hiragana is more rounded. Look at some Hiragana and Katakana charts and try to make sense of them, even if it does not work to make sense of it, it will help you when learning the actual meaning of how to use those charts later on.
  2. Use flashcards: Flashcards are a great way to practice recognizing Hiragana and Katakana characters. You can use online flashcard tools or create your own physical flashcards. For this you can use Anki or Memrise. There are other tools and even apps specifically made to learn Hiragana and Katakana.
  3. Practice writing: Writing Hiragana and Katakana characters by hand can help you remember them better. You can practice writing on blank paper or in a notebook. But be certain to use the correct stroke order. Do this for 2 reasons. First of all, they will look better. Second, Japanese people can tell if you didn’t use the correct stroke order.
  4. Use mnemonics: Mnemonics can be a helpful tool for remembering the shapes and sounds of Hiragana and Katakana characters. Could be helpful especially for Katakana as there are a lot of similar characters.
  5. Test yourself: Regularly testing yourself on the characters you’ve learned is a great way to solidify your understanding of them. You can do this by just trying to read words written in Japanese.
  6. Enjoy the experience!

I strongly recommend learning Hiragana and Katakana first before starting to learn anything else that has to do with the Japanese language. The earlier your learn them, the more used you will get to them.
