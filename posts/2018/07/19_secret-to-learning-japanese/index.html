Secret to Learning Japanese

  2018-07-19  -    Voylin's Life, Learn Japanese

The secret of learning Japanese

Japanese is a beautiful and complex language that takes time and effort to master. However, the real secret to learning Japanese is not about finding the right method or resources, but about having the right motivation. In this article, we will discuss the importance of motivation in learning a new language and offer tips on how to maintain it.

The importance of motivation

Motivation is the driving force behind any successful learning endeavor. It is what keeps us engaged and focused, and it is essential for making progress in learning a new language or achieving anything in life. Without motivation, it’s easy to become overwhelmed and give up. Even if you have been studying Japanese for so long, lack of motivation will throw away all your previous invested hours and progress. So let’s go over some points which can help to keep your motivation high!

Setting realistic goals

One of the most important things you can do to maintain your motivation is to set realistic goals. When setting goals, it’s important to be specific, measurable, and realistic. For example, instead of setting a goal to “be fluent in Japanese,” set a goal to “pass the JLPT N5 exam” first, and work your way up from that. Some other goals could be to read a manga without having to translate every single page, watching an anime episode without having to look up every single word you hear.

Celebrating small wins

Another way to maintain motivation is to celebrate small wins along the way. When you reach a milestone, no matter how small, take the time to acknowledge your accomplishment and give yourself a pat on the back. This will help you stay motivated and remind you of all the progress you’ve made. Every win is a win, no matter how small it is. One of my first wins when learning Japanese was reading the kanji of the toilet flush, 大 and 小, meaning big and small.

Finding meaningful reasons to learn

When learning a new language, it’s important to have a meaningful reason for doing so. Whether it’s for business, travel, or personal growth, having a reason for learning the language will keep you motivated and focused. We talked about setting small goals, but also have a bigger goal in mind, kind of like an end goal, with your smaller goals being the stepping stone which will take you there. Having a big overall goal will make it easier to create some small goals.

Surrounding yourself with support

Learning a new language can be lonely especially when learning a foreign language of a far away country, as the chance is big that there aren’t really any other people learning that language around you. It’s important to surround yourself with a support system of people doing the same thing as you, learning. This can be friends, family, or a language learning community. Having people to share your progress with, ask for advice, or practice speaking with can make the process more enjoyable and help you stay motivated. Luckily nowadays we have the internet, a way to connect with almost anybody around the world.

Conclusion

Learning a new language is a long-term process that requires patience, dedication, and most importantly, motivation. The real secret to learning Japanese is not about finding the perfect method or resources, but about maintaining the drive and enthusiasm to keep going. By setting realistic goals, celebrating small wins, finding meaningful reasons to learn, surrounding yourself with support, and most importantly, staying motivated, you can achieve success in your Japanese learning journey. Remember to take it step by step, don’t get discouraged if you don’t see progress right away, and enjoy the process of learning a new language!
