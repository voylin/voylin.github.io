What to do in Tennoji on a Rainy Day

  2018-08-04  -    Voylin's Life, Travel Advice

Tennoji on a rainy day

Tennoji is a vibrant neighborhood in Osaka, known for its shopping, dining, and entertainment options. While it’s a great place to explore on a sunny day, it can be equally as fun to visit on a rainy day. Here are some things to do in Tennoji on a rainy day that will keep you entertained and (hopefully) dry.

Museums

There are many museums easily accessible from Tennoji, like the Osaka Museum of Housing and Living. This museum offers a glimpse into the history and culture of Osaka, with exhibits showcasing traditional houses and daily life in the city. It really is my most favorite museum in Osaka.

Or visit the Osaka Science Museum, this museum offers a variety of interactive exhibits and hands-on activities that are perfect for a rainy day. There is also a museum about natural disasters which has a very interactive approach to it.

Visiting the Osaka Museum of History is also an option. This museum offers a fascinating look at the history of Osaka, with exhibits showcasing the city’s past and present. Or the mint museum? But I guess you probably got my point, there are many museums in Osaka and I love museums.

Shopping

Shop at Tennoji is quite easy and you can feel like an explorer, discover the large underground network leading into big shopping malls. Tennoji is basically one large shopping complex with a wide range of shops and restaurants, making it a great place to spend a rainy day.

Onsen anyone?

If you like Onsen, go to Spa World, you won’t stay dry but that’s the point. Spa World is a giant Onsen/Sento experience with 2 floors dedicated to each their own theme, Asia and Europe. These themes switch between men and women every month.

Harukas

Go to the Harukas building, there are a lot of escalators which you can take up to a certain floor and then end up in a small garden with a nice, free, view of Osaka. There is no roof on the garden so in rainy weather you better just look out of the window instead or use an umbrella.

Also try to take the stairs down when returning from the garden floor, really an experience to be in the tallest building of Japan and taking the stairs down from the highest publicly accessible area hahaha.
