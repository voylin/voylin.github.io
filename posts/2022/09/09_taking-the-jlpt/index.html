I'll take the JLPT this year

  2022-09-09  -    Voylin's Life, Our Life

What is the JLPT

The Japanese Language Proficiency Test (JLPT) is a test used to evaluate the proficiency of non-native speakers of Japanese. The test is administered by the Japan Foundation and Japan Educational Exchanges and Services.

The 5 levels:

  • N1 (The highest level)
  • N2
  • N3
  • N4
  • N5 (The lowest level) The test measures the ability to understand Japanese used in a broad range of contexts and assesses proficiency in grammar, vocabulary, reading comprehension, and listening comprehension. Everything is multiple choice and the test however fails to prove actual fluency. This test is widely accepted as proof by companies, even though this test has many issues (in my opinion).

The JLPT is held twice a year, in July and December, at designated test sites around the world. The results are not based on wether you got an answer correct or not, they add a bit of their own magic to decide who passes and loses by using their own point calculating system. This makes it so you only receive your test results 2 months later, which can be a big inconvenience to people who need it soon to get into their new job.

The JLPT is widely recognized by Japanese language schools, universities, and employers as a measure of proficiency in the language. Many students take the test as a way to track their progress in learning Japanese and to demonstrate their proficiency to potential employers or educational institutions.

Why people take the JLPT

One of the main reasons why people take the JLPT is to evaluate their proficiency in the Japanese language. The test consists of multiple parts: grammar, vocabulary, reading comprehension, and listening comprehension. This allows individuals to get a clear idea of their strengths and weaknesses in the language and to track their progress over time.

Another reason why people take the JLPT is to demonstrate their proficiency to potential employers or educational institutions. Many Japanese companies and universities require applicants to have a certain level of Japanese proficiency. By taking the JLPT, individuals can demonstrate that they possess the language skills which that company or educational institute thinks is sufficient.

For students of Japanese, the JLPT can also serve as a motivator and a goal to work towards. Because the test happens twice a year, this provides students with a clear deadline to work towards and a sense of accomplishment when they pass the test.

Lastly, taking JLPT can also be a great way to connect with other people who are also studying Japanese. Preparing for the test can be a social and supportive experience, and many students find it helpful to study with others who are also taking the test.

Why am I taking the JLPT

So after all this information, what is my personal reason on taking the JLPT. First of all, I’m going for the N3, which is sufficient for some jobs here in Japan. This also explains why I am taking it, jobs. I am strongly considering switching jobs after all past things which happened at my current job. But the biggest reason is, I want to do a job that I would prefer to do for the rest of my life.

I really like teaching kids, but it’s not a job I can do for the rest of my life and I was never planning to anyway. Especially not in the way I am teaching right now, after school teaching is something which is a lot more stressful and unstable in my opinion. If I could get a job in a real school, then I could see myself doing that job for a long time. However, I don"t have a degree to do this.

Another reason for me to take the JLPT test is to test my overal Japanese, I live in Japan but I still struggle to have conversations, make phone calls or do other things in Japan.

So this is basically why I am taking the JLPT. Good luck to all other people also taking the JLPT! ;)
